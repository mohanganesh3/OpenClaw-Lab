# EV041: Reliable Source Approximation: Source-Free Unsupervised Domain Adaptation for Vestibular Schwannoma MRI Segmentation

## Problem
Current Source-Free Unsupervised Domain Adaptation (SFUDA) approaches cannot handle complex segmentation tasks across different MRI sequences, such as vestibular schwannoma segmentation.

## Core Claim
Reliable Source Approximation (RSA) can generate source-like and structure-preserved images from the target domain for updating model parameters and adapting domain shifts.

## Key Contributions
- Proposing a novel source approximation method through edge-guided image translation
- Introducing uncertainty refinement and prediction consistency to obtain the most reliable approximation result
- Demonstrating that RSA outperforms other SFUDA methods on adaptive vestibular schwannoma segmentation

## Method
RSA deploys a conditional diffusion model to generate multiple source-like images under the guidance of varying edges of one target image, uses an uncertainty estimation module to predict and refine reliable pseudo labels, and employs prediction consistency to select the most reliable generations.
- Components: Edge-guided diffusion model for generating source-like images, Uncertainty segmentation model with NIG distribution for pseudo-label refinement, Prediction consistency mechanism for selecting optimal generations
- Key innovation: The integration of edge-guided image translation with uncertainty refinement and prediction consistency to reliably approximate source data from the target domain without requiring annotated target data.
- Assumptions: Edge guidance from the target domain can effectively preserve spatial structure when generating source-like images; Uncertainty estimation can reliably identify pixels with dependable pseudo-labels; Prediction consistency across multiple generated images under the same edge guidance indicates the quality of the approximation

## Experimental Setup
- Datasets: Public vestibular schwannoma dataset with ceT1 and hrT2 MRI sequences from 242 patients (1599 source training images, 1493 target training images, and 423 target testing images)
- Baselines: Source-only, Target-only, TENT[8], EATA[9], SAR[10], COTTA[17], FSM[22]
- Metrics: Dice similarity coefficient (Dice), Average symmetric surface distance (ASSD)
- Compute: NVIDIA A100 GPU with PyTorch platform
