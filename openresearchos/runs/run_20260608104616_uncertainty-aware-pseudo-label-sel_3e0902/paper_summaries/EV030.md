## Problem
Label deficiency and device heterogeneity in federated learning cause slow convergence and poor performance in edge networks.

## Core Claim
FedCPSL achieves fast convergence and high performance by combining pseudo-labeling and interpolation-based personalization with adaptive variance reduction.

## Key Contributions
- Novel FedCPSL algorithm for personalized semi-supervised FL
- Theoretical guarantee of sublinear convergence under heterogeneity
- Three acceleration strategies: momentum, variance reduction, normalized aggregation

## Method
FedCPSL simultaneously optimizes global model θ, pseudo-labels νi, and local models θi,lc via alternating minimization and local SGD with three acceleration strategies.
- Components: Pseudo-labeling for unlabeled data, Interpolation-based personalization, Adaptive client variance reduction, Local momentum SGD, Normalized global aggregation
- Key innovation: Adaptive client variance reduction with control variables that correct local updates while accommodating momentum and heterogeneous local updates, enabling stable convergence.
- Assumptions: Partial client participation (PCP) with small fixed-size subsets; Time-varying heterogeneous local updates (HLU); Non-IID data distribution across devices

## Experimental Setup
- Datasets: MNIST, CIFAR-10
- Baselines: FedSHVR, FedSHVRP, APFL1, APFSL

Headline: FedCPSL achieves 98.2% accuracy on MNIST (m=0.1N) and 80.7% on CIFAR-10 (m=0.1N)

- Ablations: βi study: βi=0.75 optimal for personalization (Fig 1); γ study: γ=0.8 optimal for momentum (Fig 2); HLU robustness: FedCPSL degrades least under HLU (Fig 5, Table I)
- Improvement over baseline: FedCPSL reduces communication rounds to reach 95% accuracy by up to 60% compared to APFL/APFSL (30 rounds vs 80+). Under HLU, FedCPSL outperforms FedSHVR by 24.5% on MNIST (97.5% vs 73.0%).
