# EV045: Scribble-based 3D Multiple Abdominal Organ Segmentation via Triple-branch Multi-dilated Network with Pixel- and Class-wise Consistency

## Problem
Closing the performance gap between weakly-supervised scribble-based and fully-supervised methods for 3D abdominal organ segmentation in CT images.

## Core Claim
The proposed TDNet with dual consistency constraints (USPC and MPCC) achieves state-of-the-art performance in scribble-supervised abdominal organ segmentation.

## Key Contributions
- A novel 3D Triple-branch Multi-dilated Network (TDNet) with one encoder and three decoders using dilation rates of 1, 3, and 6 to capture complementary multi-scale features.
- Uncertainty-weighted Soft Pseudo label Consistency (USPC) loss to rectify noisy pseudo labels using voxel-wise entropy-based uncertainty weighting.
- Multi-view Projection-based Class-similarity Consistency (MPCC) loss to enforce class relationship consistency across axial, sagittal, and coronal views.

## Method
TDNet uses a shared encoder feeding three decoders with dilation rates 1/3/6. Decoder outputs are averaged into soft pseudo labels, weighted by uncertainty (entropy) via USPC loss, and class affinity matrices are aligned across multi-view projections via MPCC loss, supervised alongside partial cross-entropy on scribbles.
- Components: Triple-branch Multi-dilated Network (TDNet) with dilation rates 1, 3, 6, Uncertainty-weighted Soft Pseudo label Consistency (USPC) loss using entropy-based weights, Multi-view Projection-based Class-similarity Consistency (MPCC) loss using class affinity matrices, Partial Cross-Entropy (pCE) loss for scribble supervision
- Key innovation: Voxel-wise uncertainty weighting in USPC loss to suppress unreliable pseudo labels and multi-view projection in MPCC loss to leverage inter-class relationships, jointly stabilizing pseudo-label generation and improving segmentation.
- Assumptions: Different dilation rates capture complementary multi-scale features; Class affinity matrices computed via multi-view projection encode meaningful inter-class relationships; Entropy-based uncertainty estimation effectively identifies unreliable pseudo labels

## Experimental Setup
- Datasets: WORD dataset (cervical cancer patients before radiotherapy, 100 training, 20 validation, 30 testing)
- Baselines: pCE [12], Total Variation Loss (TV) [9], USTM [13], EM [8], DMPLS [15], FullySup [3]
- Metrics: Dice Similarity Coefficient (DSC), 95% Hausdorff Distance (HD95), Average Surface Distance (ASD)
- Compute: single GPU (NVIDIA 2080Ti with 11GB memory)

## Critical Reading
- Stated limitations: Performance gap between weakly-supervised and fully supervised methods; Limited annotation efficiency compared to fully supervised approaches; Potential challenges with very small or anatomically complex organs
- Unstated weaknesses: Limited ablation studies on individual components of the triple-branch network; Potential overfitting to specific dataset characteristics; Lack of comparison with more recent weakly supervised methods; Unclear computational complexity and training requirements
- Threats to validity: Dataset bias - method may not generalize to different CT scanners or protocols; Annotation bias - scribbles may not capture all relevant anatomical variations; Domain shift challenges when applied to different patient populations
- Open questions: How does the method perform with different levels of scribble annotation quality?; What is the minimum amount of scribble information needed for acceptable performance?; Can the approach be effectively adapted to other imaging modalities beyond CT?; How does the method handle cases with significant anatomical variations?
