# EV012: Enhancing Prediction Confidence in Multi-Source Source-Free Domain Adaptation for Fault Diagnosis

## Abstract

In this paper, we propose a novel multi-source source-free domain adaptation method, EPCSF, designed to address the challenges of fault diagnosis in industrial scenarios where source data is inaccessible due to privacy or storage constraints. By leveraging a global contrastive learning framework with a dynamic memory bank and a confidence-driven uncertainty suppression strategy, the proposed method enhances both feature discriminability and prediction reliability in the target domain. Specifically, it incorporates entropy minimization and mutual information maximization to stabilize pseudo-label learning and avoid model collapse. Extensive experiments conducted on two benchmark datasets, CWRU and TPF, demonstrate that EPCSF consistently outperforms existing source-free methods in terms of accuracy, robustness, and generalization. These results validate the effectiveness of EPCSF in realizing reliable knowledge transfer and high -confidence cross-domain fault classification without using any source domain data. Compared with existing SFDA methods, EPCSF introduces a novel integration of global memory-based contrastive learning and dual-mode confidence optimization, which enables more reliable pseudo-labeling and feature discrimination without access to any source data.
