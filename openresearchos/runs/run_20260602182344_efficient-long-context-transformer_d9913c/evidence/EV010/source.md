## Abstract

Semiconductor wafer inspection is recognized as a critical process for ensuring chip yield and performance, withitstechnological advancement directly influencing manufacturing efficiency and product quality in the semiconductorindustry. To overcome the limitations of conventional defect detection methods—including low efficiency, unstableresults, and inadequate multi-class defect identification—an optical microscope is employed to acquire wafer surfacedefect data. By improving the YOLOv8 algorithm, a deep learning detection framework SG-YOLO based on improvedYOLOv8 is proposed. The addition of the SimAM attention mechanism, which utilizes its property of not needingtointroduce additional parameters, results in a significant reduction in computational complexity and improves theaccuracy of small target detection. Meanwhile, the introduction of Gold-YOLO model realizes the efficient aggregationand dynamic distribution of cross-layer features to solve the traditional information loss problem. Experiments showthat the proposed method can quickly recognize different kinds of defects with good robustness. The average recognitionaccuracy of defects reaches 92.7%, which is 10.5% and 7.6% higher compared to YOLOv8n and YOLOv8s, respectively. Thus the proposed SG-YOLOv8 model has some potential in the field of semiconductor defect detection.
