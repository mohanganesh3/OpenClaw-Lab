## Abstract

Micro-defect detection in cigarette packaging is critical for ensuring product quality and maintaining brand reputation. However, traditional defect detection methods are limited in their ability to efficiently and accurately identify complex defects, especially under diverse operational conditions. This study presents an attention-mechanism-based model designed to enhance micro-defect detection in cigarette packages. The proposed method utilizes advanced convolutional neural networks (CNNs) combined with attention mechanisms to enhance feature extraction and defect localization. Experimental results confirm the model's superior performance in detection accuracy, processing speed, and robustness, surpassing existing state-of-the-art techniques. These findings demonstrate the approach's potential for industrial applications and its significant contribution to advancing defect detection technologies.
