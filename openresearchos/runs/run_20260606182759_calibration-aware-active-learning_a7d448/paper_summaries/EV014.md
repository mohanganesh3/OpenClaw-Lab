## Problem
Current machine learning potentials lack reliable uncertainty quantification, limiting their use in active learning, calibration, and out-of-distribution detection.

## Core Claim
Bayesian E(3)-equivariant MLPs with iterative restratification and joint energy-force uncertainty modeling achieve competitive accuracy while enabling uncertainty-guided applications.

## Key Contributions
- Development of Bayesian E(3)-equivariant MLPs with iterative restratification of many-body message passing
- Introduction of joint energy-force negative log-likelihood (NLLJEF) loss function for uncertainty modeling
- Systematic benchmarking of multiple Bayesian approaches (deep ensembles, SWAG, IVON) on uncertainty tasks
- Demonstration of efficient active learning using BALD with energy-force uncertainty quantification

## Method
They develop Bayesian E(3)-equivariant MLPs using iterative restratification of many-body message passing, trained with a joint energy-force negative log-likelihood loss function that explicitly models uncertainties in both energies and forces.
- Components: Bayesian neural network architecture with E(3)-equivariance, Iterative restratification of many-body message passing, Joint energy-force uncertainty modeling via NLLJEF loss, Multiple Bayesian approaches (deep ensembles, SWAG, IVON, LLPR)
- Key innovation: The NLLJEF loss function that simultaneously optimizes for energy and force uncertainties, reducing energy errors by 3-4 times and force errors by 2-3 times compared to energy-only uncertainty modeling while maintaining competitive accuracy.
- Assumptions: Joint modeling of energy and force uncertainties is more effective than energy-only uncertainty modeling; E(3)-equivariance is essential for physical consistency in interatomic potentials; Bayesian approaches can provide practical uncertainty estimates for active learning applications
