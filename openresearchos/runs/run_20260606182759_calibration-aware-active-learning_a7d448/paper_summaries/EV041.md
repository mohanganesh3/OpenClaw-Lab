## Problem
The paper addresses the gap in analyzing QR factorization algorithms under imperfect orthonormality and Gaussian noise, where prior work assumed exact arithmetic and perfect orthogonality.

## Core Claim
The authors derive bounds on condition number growth for matrices without assuming perfect orthonormality, enabling analysis of QR methods under imperfect orthogonalization and Gaussian noise.

## Key Contributions
- General bounds on condition number growth when adding columns without orthonormality assumptions (Theorem 3.2)
- Probabilistic analysis of vector norms under Gaussian noise using non-central chi-squared distributions (Theorem 4.3)
- Analysis of orthogonal projections under Gaussian noise (Theorem 5.1)

## Method
The approach combines deterministic matrix analysis (Lemma 3.1) with probabilistic tools (Bessel functions, Marcum Q-function) to bound condition number growth and noise propagation.
- Components: Lemma 3.1: Eigenvalue bounds for block matrices, Theorem 3.2: Condition number bounds for column addition, Theorem 4.3: Probability distribution of noisy vector norms, Theorem 5.1: Orthogonal projection under Gaussian noise
- Key innovation: The key innovation is removing the orthonormality assumption in condition number analysis by incorporating Gaussian noise models and probabilistic bounds.
- Assumptions: Gaussian noise with i.i.d. components Yi ∼ N(0, σ²); Matrix B has full column rank (implied by σ²min(B) > 0); Added vector x ∈ span(B⊥)
