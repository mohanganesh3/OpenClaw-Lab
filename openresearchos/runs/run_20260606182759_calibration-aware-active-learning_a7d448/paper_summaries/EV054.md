## Problem
The paper addresses the gap between score estimation (core to SGMs/DDPMs) and classical distribution learning forms like parameter estimation and density estimation.

## Core Claim
The paper introduces a framework that reduces score estimation to parameter and density estimation, establishing new connections and implications in statistical and computational learning theory.

## Key Contributions
- Parameter estimation: Shows denoising score matching in DDPMs is asymptotically efficient (unlike implicit score matching), with covariance given by inverse Fisher information
- Density estimation: Lifts estimated scores to (ε,δ)-PAC density estimators, achieving minimax rates for Hölder classes and quasi-polynomial algorithms for Gaussian mixtures
- Lower bounds: Provides computational lower bounds for score estimation, proving cryptographic hardness for Gaussian mixtures

## Method
The approach uses the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process and establishes connections via Lemma 1 (linking log-likelihood to DDPM score matching) and a reduction framework that transforms score estimation accuracy to density estimation guarantees.
- Components: Lemma 1: Proves relationship between log-likelihood and integrated score matching objective using Fokker-Planck equation and Ito's formula, Reduction framework: Transforms score estimation to PAC density estimation using Markov's inequality, Lower bound technique: Uses cryptographic hardness of Gaussian pancake problem
- Key innovation: The core innovation is the general reduction framework that converts score estimation accuracy to density estimation guarantees, enabling transfer of statistical/computational results between domains.
- Assumptions: Distributions are absolutely continuous w.r.t. Lebesgue measure in Rd; Mild regularity conditions for asymptotic efficiency; Access to accurate score estimates
