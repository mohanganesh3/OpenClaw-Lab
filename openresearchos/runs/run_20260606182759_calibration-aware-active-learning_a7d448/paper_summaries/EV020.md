## Problem
Deterministic embeddings from standard vision-language models struggle to capture uncertainties from ambiguities in visual/textual descriptions and multiple image-text correspondences, while existing probabilistic approaches require retraining VLMs with large datasets.

## Core Claim
GroVE provides a post-hoc approach to obtain probabilistic embeddings from frozen VLMs using Gaussian Process Latent Variable Models, achieving state-of-the-art uncertainty calibration across downstream tasks.

## Key Contributions
- Proposes GroVE as a post-hoc method for uncertainty quantification in frozen VLMs
- Develops a GPLVM-based framework with single-modal reconstruction and cross-modal alignment objectives
- Demonstrates superior uncertainty calibration performance across cross-modal retrieval, VQA, and active learning tasks

## Method
GroVE builds on Gaussian Process Latent Variable Models to learn a low-dimensional latent space where image and text inputs are mapped to unified representations, optimized through single-modal embedding reconstruction and cross-modal alignment objectives.
- Components: Gaussian Process Latent Variable Model (GPLVM) for probabilistic embedding, Single-modal embedding reconstruction objective, Cross-modal alignment objective
- Key innovation: Using GPLVM to model uncertainty in the latent space of frozen VLM embeddings, enabling uncertainty quantification without retraining the original model by capturing both latent space uncertainty and prediction noise.
- Assumptions: Frozen VLM embeddings provide meaningful initial representations; A low-dimensional latent space can capture essential structure and uncertainties; Single-modal reconstruction and cross-modal alignment objectives are sufficient for learning uncertainty-aware embeddings

## Critical Reading
- Stated limitations: Deterministic embeddings from standard VLMs struggle to capture uncertainties arising from ambiguities in visual and textual descriptions and multiple possible correspondences between images and texts; Existing approaches for probabilistic embeddings require large datasets and don't leverage the powerful representations already learned by large-scale VLMs like CLIP
- Unstated weaknesses: The post-hoc nature of the approach might limit the quality of probabilistic embeddings compared to models trained with probabilistic objectives from scratch; Computational efficiency and scalability of GPLVM for large datasets and models are not addressed; Domain mismatch issues when applying VLMs trained on general images to specialized domains like medical imaging
- Threats to validity: The approach might not generalize well to domains significantly different from the pre-training data of the VLMs; The quality of probabilistic embeddings depends on the quality of the original VLM representations; Uncertainty quantification might be less accurate for out-of-distribution samples
- Open questions: How well does this approach scale to very large datasets?; How does the quality of probabilistic embeddings compare to models trained with probabilistic objectives from scratch?; How does this approach perform with different types of VLMs or in different domains?; What are the computational requirements and feasibility for practical applications?

## Gaps This Enables
- Leveraging powerful pre-trained VLMs for uncertainty quantification in domains where fine-tuning is difficult or expensive
- Obtaining calibrated uncertainties from deterministic models, which could be valuable for medical applications where uncertainty estimation is crucial
- Applying probabilistic embeddings to active learning scenarios in medical imaging
