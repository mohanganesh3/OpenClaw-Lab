## Critical Reading
- Stated limitations: Existing machine learning approaches rely on deterministic mappings from composition-derived features to phase labels, neglecting the uncertainty inherent in such predictions; The paper addresses this by using Mixture Density Networks (MDNs) to predict phase fractions and quantify aleatoric uncertainty
- Unstated weaknesses: High domain specificity to refractory multi-principal element alloys with unclear transferability to medical imaging; No mention of dataset size, quality, or potential biases in the data; Computational requirements and scalability of the approach not specified; Limited discussion of model interpretability and explainability
- Threats to validity: Domain specificity threatens generalization to medical imaging applications; Performance at extreme temperatures not explicitly evaluated; Active learning for out-of-domain discovery may not translate well to medical imaging; Uncertainty quantification method may not be optimal for all types of uncertainty
- Open questions: How well does the MDN approach generalize to other domains like medical imaging?; What is the computational cost of training and deploying these models?; How does the active learning component perform with different types of uncertainty?; What is the impact of the size and quality of the training data on model performance?
