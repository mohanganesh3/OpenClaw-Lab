## Problem
Efficient uniform sampling of models for first-order logic sentences on finite domains, specifically extending previous results from the universally-quantified subfragment (UFO2) to the entire two-variable fragment (FO2).

## Core Claim
FO2 is domain-liftable under sampling, meaning there exists a polynomial-time sampling algorithm in the domain size, even in the presence of counting constraints.

## Key Contributions
- Proving domain-liftability under sampling for the entire FO2 fragment
- Extending results to handle counting constraints (e.g., ∀x∃=ky:ϕ(x,y) and ∃=kx∀y:ϕ(x,y))
- Providing a constructive sampling method with applications in combinatorial structures and statistical-relational models

## Method
Uses Scott Normal Form (SNF) to transform FO2 sentences, introduces Tseitin predicates to handle existential quantifiers, applies a domain recursion scheme with partitioning based on 1-types and 2-tables, and employs enumerative sampling for partition sizes.
- Components: SNF transformation with auxiliary predicates, Domain recursion scheme with substructure sampling, Partitioning based on block and cell types, Enumerative sampling for partition sizes
- Key innovation: The domain recursion scheme that decomposes the sampling problem into smaller subproblems by iteratively sampling substructures and updating domain constraints, enabling polynomial-time complexity.
- Assumptions: Function-free first-order sentences; Symmetric weights (determined solely by relation symbols); Finite domains; Sentences in Scott Normal Form
