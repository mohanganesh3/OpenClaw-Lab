## Abstract

As cloud infrastructures become increasingly critical to business operations, the demand for resilient, scalable, and autonomous disaster recovery (DR) mechanisms has intensified. Manual DR processes are inadequate in meeting the low-latency recovery expectations of modern enterprises. This paper introduces a fully automated disaster recovery architecture tailored for cloud environments, integrating Infrastructure as Code (IaC), event-driven orchestration, and policy-based failover strategies. By leveraging cloud-native services and automation pipelines, the proposed framework achieves rapid system restoration with minimal human intervention. We validate the approach using fault-injection testing across multi-cloud environments, benchmarking improvements in Recovery Time Objective (RTO) and Recovery Point Objective (RPO) compared to conventional DR solutions. Results indicate up to a 65% reduction in recovery time and enhanced operational continuity. This work underscores the transformative role of automation in elevating disaster resilience and reliability in cloud computing.
