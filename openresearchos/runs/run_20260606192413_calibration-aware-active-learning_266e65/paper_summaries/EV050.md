## Problem
Conventional geothermal reservoir modeling approaches are inefficient for uncertainty quantification as they require estimating unknown model parameters before making predictions.

## Core Claim
Data Space Inversion (DSI) provides a robust and efficient alternative for making predictions with quantified uncertainty by avoiding direct parameter estimation entirely.

## Key Contributions
- Introduction of DSI as an alternative methodology for geothermal reservoir modeling that bypasses parameter estimation
- Development of a modified DSI algorithm that directly samples from the posterior predictive distribution
- Demonstration of DSI's accuracy and efficiency through two geothermal reservoir modeling case studies

## Method
DSI constructs a surrogate mapping from Gaussian samples to the joint distribution of data and predictive quantities by approximating marginal CDFs, applying Cholesky factorization for Gaussian approximation, and using triangular transformations for posterior sampling.
- Components: Sample generation from prior predictive distribution using forward model simulations, Construction of piecewise linear CDF approximations for each data and prediction variable, Application of inverse transformation and Cholesky factorization to approximate joint distribution as Gaussian, Triangular transformation structure enabling direct posterior predictive sampling
- Key innovation: The triangular transformation structure that allows direct sampling from the posterior predictive distribution without parameter estimation by leveraging the monotonicity and lower-triangular properties of the constructed surrogate mapping.
- Assumptions: The joint distribution of the transformed variables can be reasonably approximated as Gaussian; The transformation functions are strictly increasing on their respective domains; The covariance matrix is full rank (with possible regularization via identity matrix addition)
