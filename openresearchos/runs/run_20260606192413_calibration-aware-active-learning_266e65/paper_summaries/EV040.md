## Problem
Training video diffusion models from scratch is computationally expensive and resource-intensive, limiting accessibility for applications requiring realistic spatiotemporal dynamics.

## Core Claim
A time-consistency discriminator can guide pretrained image diffusion models to generate realistic time-consistent dynamics without requiring model finetuning or extensions.

## Key Contributions
- Proposed a time-consistency discriminator that guides sampling inference of pretrained image diffusion models
- Demonstrated superior uncertainty calibration and lower biases compared to video diffusion models
- Achieved stable centennial-scale climate simulations at daily time steps

## Method
The method trains a time-consistency discriminator to evaluate temporal coherence between consecutive frames during sampling, then incorporates this discriminator's guidance signal to steer the diffusion process toward temporally consistent outputs without modifying the base image diffusion model.
- Components: Pretrained image diffusion model as the base generator, Time-consistency discriminator trained to evaluate temporal coherence, Guidance mechanism that incorporates discriminator feedback during sampling
- Key innovation: The discriminator-guided sampling mechanism that penalizes temporally inconsistent predictions during the diffusion process, enabling time consistency without model finetuning.
- Assumptions: The pretrained image diffusion model has reasonable spatial generation capabilities; The discriminator can effectively learn temporal consistency patterns from training data; The guidance signal can be incorporated without destabilizing the diffusion process
