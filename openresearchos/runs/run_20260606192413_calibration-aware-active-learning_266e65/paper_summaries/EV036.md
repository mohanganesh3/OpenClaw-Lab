## Problem
Overconfident predictions from one-hot labels lead to poor robustness under noise and perturbations in image classification.

## Core Claim
A hybrid quantum-classical framework using quantum non-determinism can generate nuanced probabilistic labels that improve robustness while maintaining accuracy.

## Key Contributions
- Variational quantum circuit (VQC) for encoding inputs into quantum states capturing feature correlations
- Quantum-derived soft labels via Born rule measurement reflecting input-specific uncertainty
- Demonstrated up to 50% higher accuracy under noise on MNIST/Fashion-MNIST benchmarks

## Method
Encode inputs into multi-qubit quantum states using VQC with entanglement/superposition, extract probabilistic labels via quantum measurement, train classical CNNs with soft-target cross-entropy loss.
- Components: Variational quantum circuit (VQC) encoding, Born rule measurement for label extraction, Classical CNN training with quantum-derived soft labels
- Key innovation: Quantum non-determinism enabling natural representation of input-specific ambiguity through superposition/entanglement, producing human-like uncertainty distributions.
- Assumptions: Quantum states can effectively represent image feature correlations; Probabilistic labels better reflect real-world classification uncertainty; Gradient-based optimization of VQC parameters is feasible
