## Problem
Keeping digital twins of natural systems aligned with physical systems that evolve over time, are only partially observed, and are modeled by mechanistic simulators with unmeasurable parameters, where sparse observations often fail to identify unique optimal calibrations.

## Core Claim
GFlowNets provide a superior approach to model adaptation by formulating it as a generative modeling problem over complete simulator configurations, allowing sampling of plausible parameterizations with probability proportional to their agreement with observed behavior.

## Key Contributions
- Formulation of model adaptation as a generative modeling problem over complete simulator configurations
- Development of a GFlowNet-based approach that samples plausible parameterizations with probability proportional to reward derived from agreement with observations
- Demonstration through agriculture case study that the approach recovers dominant regions of adaptation landscape, retrieves strong calibration hypotheses, and preserves multiple plausible configurations under uncertainty

## Method
The method formulates model adaptation as a structured parameter-set construction problem where a GFlowNet sequentially constructs complete simulator configurations through decisions over parameter groups, with reward derived from contextual discrepancy between simulated and observed behavior using a tail-sensitive score for robustness.
- Components: GFlowNet architecture that constructs simulator configurations sequentially through decisions over parameter groups, Reward function based on contextual discrepancy between simulated and observed behavior, with tail-sensitive scoring, Training procedure that concentrates probability mass along branches leading to higher-reward parameterizations while preserving diversity
- Key innovation: Formulating model adaptation as a generative modeling problem over complete simulator configurations using GFlowNets, which allows direct sampling of plausible parameterizations rather than just characterizing posterior distributions, preserving uncertainty while providing directly usable configurations.
- Assumptions: The mechanistic simulator can be evaluated for any parameterization; Observations are available for multiple contexts; The parameter space can be discretized through a perturbation scheme; The discrepancy measure D(·,·) can be computed between simulated and observed trajectories

## Experimental Setup
- Datasets: simulator for natural systems (dry-mass measurements)

- Metrics: mode discovery accuracy, ranked probability profile alignment, top-k recovery, best-so-far performance
