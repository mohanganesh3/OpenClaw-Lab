## Problem
Reliable task-specific ranking of LLMs from sparse and imbalanced pairwise comparisons, where global leaderboards mask task heterogeneity and per-task estimates are statistically unstable.

## Core Claim
A low-rank framework for task-specific LLM ranking can share information across related tasks while preserving task-specific differences, enabling uncertainty-aware certification under sparse comparisons.

## Key Contributions
- Development of a max-norm accurate estimator for latent score matrices with task-wise top-K recovery guarantees under sparse sampling
- Construction of debiased one-step estimators for fixed score contrasts yielding asymptotically valid confidence intervals attaining semiparametric efficiency lower bound
- Extension to high-dimensional ranking regime with simultaneous confidence sets for per-task ranks and valid tests for top-K membership across many tasks and models

## Method
The method models the task-by-model ability matrix as low-rank to share information across related tasks, develops max-norm accurate estimators for score matrices, constructs debiased estimators for score contrasts, and uses Gaussian and multiplier-bootstrap calibration for simultaneous inference across tasks.
- Components: Low-rank assumption for task-by-model ability matrix, Max-norm accurate estimator for latent score matrix, Debiased one-step estimators for fixed score contrasts, Multiplier-bootstrap calibration for simultaneous confidence sets
- Key innovation: The low-rank structure that enables information sharing across related tasks while preserving task-specific differences, allowing improved sample efficiency and uncertainty quantification in sparse regimes.
- Assumptions: The task-by-model ability matrix is approximately low-rank; Pairwise comparisons follow a Bradley-Terry model; Sufficient regularity conditions for asymptotic results; The number of latent dimensions r is known or can be estimated

## Critical Reading
- Stated limitations: Global leaderboards mask task heterogeneity; Independent task-specific ranking is statistically unstable under sparse and imbalanced comparisons
- Unstated weaknesses: Uncertainty quantification framework details are incomplete in the abstract; No discussion of calibration requirements for medical applications; Limited exploration of computational efficiency for large-scale applications; No consideration of domain-specific challenges in medical imaging
- Threats to validity: The low-rank assumption may not hold for diverse medical imaging tasks; Potential bias in pairwise comparisons if certain models are overrepresented; Framework may not account for critical importance of calibration in medical applications
- Open questions: How does the uncertainty quantification framework translate to confidence intervals?; Can the method be adapted for medical image classification where calibration is critical?; How well does the low-rank assumption hold for highly specialized medical tasks?; What are the computational requirements for scaling to large numbers of models and tasks?
