## Problem
Existing multi-domain skin lesion segmentation models inadequately utilize prediction uncertainty, leading to 'blind fusion' of cross-domain artifacts, 'passive' topological reasoning, and 'rigid optimization' that overfits to subjective label noise.

## Core Claim
UGDD-Net's uncertainty-in-the-loop unified architecture with 'Glance-and-Gaze' forward mechanism transforms uncertainty into an active guiding signal, achieving more reliable skin lesion segmentation than existing methods.

## Key Contributions
- Uncertainty-Guided Dual-Domain Network (UGDD-Net) with novel two-pass 'Glance-and-Gaze' forward mechanism
- Three uncertainty-guided modules: UGBFF for dynamic feature fusion, UGGR for topology-aware graph refinement, and UGML for margin-adaptive loss calculation

## Method
UGDD-Net employs a dual-domain architecture with spatial and frequency branches, utilizing a two-pass 'Glance-and-Gaze' forward mechanism where uncertainty actively modulates spatial-spectral interactions, enables priority-guided topology-aware reasoning, and enforces adaptive loss constraints.
- Components: Uncertainty-Guided Bi-directional Feature Fusion (UGBFF) module, Uncertainty-Guided Graph Refine (UGGR) module, Uncertainty-Guided Margin-Adaptive Loss (UGML)
- Key innovation: The 'Glance-and-Gaze' forward mechanism that actively utilizes pixel-level uncertainty to dynamically modulate feature fusion, construct priority-guided topology-aware graphs, and enforce margin-adaptive loss constraints.
- Assumptions: Prediction uncertainty can be effectively estimated and utilized as an active guiding signal; Dual-domain (spatial and frequency) approach provides complementary information for robust segmentation; Graph-based reasoning can effectively propagate reliable semantic consensus to refine uncertain nodes

## Critical Reading
- Stated limitations: The abstract doesn't explicitly state limitations, but given the focus on skin lesion segmentation, potential limitations could include performance on lesions with atypical appearances or in cases with poor image quality.
- Unstated weaknesses: Potential overfitting to specific lesion types or imaging conditions; Limited generalizability across different medical imaging modalities; Unclear handling of class imbalance in lesion segmentation; Potential computational inefficiency for real-time clinical applications
- Threats to validity: Limited external validation on datasets from different institutions or imaging devices; Possible dataset bias if training data comes from a single source; Uncertain performance on rare lesion types or early-stage lesions
- Open questions: How does the uncertainty guidance mechanism specifically improve segmentation reliability?; What is the impact of dual-domain learning on segmentation performance compared to single-domain approaches?; How does the method perform on different types of skin lesions beyond the specific types studied?
