# EV012: Surviving Sepsis Campaign: International guidelines for management of severe sepsis and septic shock: 2008

Year: 2007

URL: https://doi.org/10.1007/s00134-007-0934-2

## Core Claim

The Surviving Sepsis Campaign provides updated international guidelines for managing severe sepsis and septic shock through evidence-based recommendations developed via consensus methods, emphasizing early goal-directed resuscitation and categorized interventions for critical care.

## Method Or Focus

- Modified Delphi method
- consensus conference with 55 international experts
- GRADE system for evidence quality assessment
- subgroup meetings and teleconferences
- electronic-based discussion

## Limitations To Track

- No industry funding but potential bias from expert selection
- GRADE system subjectivity in evidence interpretation
- Lack of direct patient data in recommendation development

## Possible Gap

Guidelines rely on expert consensus where evidence quality is low (GRADE C-D), leaving uncertainty in areas like pediatric-specific protocols and resource-limited settings.
