# EV005: Addressing Data Scarcity in 3D Trauma Detection through Self-Supervised and Semi-Supervised Learning with Vertex Relative Position Encoding

## Problem
The severe scarcity of annotated medical data for 3D trauma detection in abdominal CT scans.

## Core Claim
Self-supervised pre-training combined with semi-supervised learning can effectively address label scarcity in medical imaging, enabling robust 3D object detection with limited annotations.

## Key Contributions
- Patch-based Masked Image Modeling (MIM) for self-supervised pre-training of a 3D U-Net encoder on 1,206 unlabeled CT volumes.
- 3D Vertex Relative Position Encoding (3D RPE) for 3D injury detection that overcomes limitations of center-based distance metrics.

## Method
The approach uses patch-based Masked Image Modeling to pre-train a 3D U-Net encoder on unlabeled CT volumes, then employs this encoder for downstream tasks including 3D injury detection with VDETR and 3D RPE, and multi-label injury classification.
- Components: Patch-based Masked Image Modeling (MIM) for self-supervised pre-training., 3D U-Net encoder architecture., VDETR with 3D Vertex Relative Position Encoding for detection., Semi-supervised learning with consistency regularization.
- Key innovation: The 3D Vertex Relative Position Encoding (3D RPE) mechanism, which computes geometric relationships between each voxel and all eight corners of the predicted bounding box, addressing the insufficiency of center-based distance metrics in 3D medical imaging.
- Assumptions: The pre-trained encoder features are transferable to downstream tasks.; Masked reconstruction provides sufficient supervision for learning anatomical representations.; Semi-supervised learning with consistency regularization can leverage unlabeled data effectively.

## Experimental Setup
- Datasets: RSNA 2023 dataset (3,147 patients, 3,206 CT series for classification), 206 labeled CT volumes with 3D bounding box annotations for 3D injury detection (144 train, 30 val, 32 test), 2,000 unlabeled CT volumes for semi-supervised learning
- Baselines: Supervised-only training, Semi-supervised learning with consistency regularization
- Metrics: Mean Average Precision (mAP) at IoU thresholds 0.10, 0.25, 0.50, 0.75, Accuracy (Acc), Area Under ROC Curve (AUC), Mean Squared Error (MSE), Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio (PSNR)
- Compute: NVIDIA A100 GPUs

## Results
Headline: Semi-supervised learning achieved 56.57% mAP@0.50 (114% improvement) for 3D injury detection

- Ablations: Semi-supervised learning vs. supervised-only training; Semi-supervised learning provides stable convergence vs. catastrophic performance collapse in supervised-only; Consistency regularization improves bounding box localization accuracy
- Improvement over baseline: 114% improvement in mAP@0.50 (from 26.36% to 56.57%) and 562% improvement in mAP@0.75 (from 6.82% to 45.12%)

## Critical Reading

- Unstated weaknesses: No explicit handling of label noise in the semi-supervised framework, which is central to the project topic; Limited to 3D trauma detection in abdominal CT scans, with unclear generalization to other medical imaging tasks; Unclear computational requirements and scalability for larger datasets; No mention of uncertainty quantification in the semi-supervised learning process; Potential dataset-specific biases not addressed in the evaluation
- Threats to validity: Limited external validity due to single-dataset evaluation; Potential overfitting to specific trauma patterns in the training data; Uncertainty about how the approach would perform under varying degrees of label noise; Limited assessment of clinical applicability and integration into workflows
