# EV003: Sam-Driven Weakly Supervised Nodule Segmentation with Uncertainty-Aware Cross Teaching

## Problem
Existing nodule segmentation methods require costly and labor-intensive pixel-level annotations, limiting their practical application in ultrasound imaging.

## Core Claim
A weakly supervised framework leveraging SAM's pseudo-label generation with uncertainty-aware cross-teaching can achieve accurate nodule segmentation using only aspect ratio annotations.

## Key Contributions
- Three types of bounding box prompts generated from clinical shape priors to enhance SAM's segmentation capabilities
- SAM-driven uncertainty estimation method using prediction inconsistencies to identify unreliable regions
- Uncertainty-aware cross-teaching strategy integrating label-space perturbations to mitigate pseudo-label noise

## Method
The framework first generates three bounding box prompts (tight box, ellipse-based, and circle-based) from clinical annotations to guide SAM in producing pseudo-labels. It then selects optimal pseudo-labels using intersection/union operations and employs uncertainty-aware cross-teaching between two models trained on these labels.
- Components: Box Prompts Generation (BPG) module creating shape-prior bounding boxes, Pseudo-label selection module using intersection/union operations, SAM-driven uncertainty estimation computing XOR between intersection and union masks, Uncertainty-aware cross-teaching with dual models and label-space perturbations
- Key innovation: Using SAM's prediction inconsistencies (via XOR operation between intersection and union of pseudo-labels) to generate uncertainty maps that guide cross-teaching, effectively reducing the impact of pseudo-label inaccuracies during training.
- Assumptions: Nodules exhibit convex shapes that can be approximated by ellipses and circles; SAM's predictions, despite prompt sensitivity, contain sufficient signal for uncertainty estimation; Cross-teaching between two models can effectively leverage complementary pseudo-label information

## Experimental Setup
- Datasets: Thyroid Ultrasound Dataset (422 left + 422 right images), Breast Ultrasound Dataset (604 left + 151 right images)
- Baselines: GrabCut [13], Entropy Minimization (EM) [1], Total Variation (TV) loss [2], Mumford-Shah loss [3], GatedCRF Loss [16], WSSS [12], RECISTSup [11], CoTraining [9], CoTeaching [17], TriNet [18], MTCL [19], SAM [14], Fully Supervised
- Metrics: Dice similarity coefficient (DSC), 95th percentile Hausdorff Distance (HD95)
- Compute: NVIDIA RTX 3090 GPU

## Critical Reading
- Stated limitations: —
- Unstated weaknesses: Dependence on SAM foundation model which may not be optimized for medical images; Limited annotation type (only aspect ratio annotations) may not capture complex nodule shapes; Single modality focus (ultrasound) raises questions about cross-modality generalization; Uncertainty estimation mechanism not elaborated in abstract; Lack of comparison with other weakly supervised methods
- Threats to validity: Potential overfitting to specific ultrasound datasets; SAM's performance on medical images may differ significantly from natural images; Quality of aspect ratio annotations could significantly impact performance; Uncertainty estimates may not be calibrated properly for medical applications
- Open questions: How exactly is uncertainty quantified and used in the cross teaching process?; What is the computational cost of using SAM for pseudo-label generation?; How does the method perform with different types of nodules or imaging artifacts?; What is the threshold for pseudo-label selection based on uncertainty?
