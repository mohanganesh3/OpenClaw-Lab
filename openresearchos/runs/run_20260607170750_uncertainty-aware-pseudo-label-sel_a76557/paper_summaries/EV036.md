## Problem
Existing semi-supervised learning methods do not incorporate uncertainty quantification for pseudo-labels or unlabeled samples, leading to issues with noisy labels and out-of-distribution samples due to strong augmentations.

## Core Claim
Complementing SSL with an uncertainty-aware objective that quantifies both aleatoric and epistemic uncertainty improves performance by aligning uncertainty distributions between labeled and unlabeled domains.

## Key Contributions
- Proposing a novel uncertainty-aware objective incorporating both aleatoric and epistemic uncertainty for SSL
- Implementing the approach by complementing contemporary SSL work with the uncertainty framework
- Achieving state-of-the-art performance on standard SSL benchmarks including CIFAR-100 and Mini-ImageNet

## Method
The approach proposes an unsupervised uncertainty-aware objective that quantifies aleatoric and epistemic uncertainty to align distributions between labeled and unlabeled domains, complementing existing SSL techniques without requiring computationally expensive graph formation or similarity computations.
- Components: Pseudo-labeling mechanism with confidence thresholding, Consistency regularization between weakly and strongly augmented samples, Uncertainty quantification using both aleatoric and epistemic measures
- Key innovation: Incorporating uncertainty quantification into the SSL training process to handle noisy pseudo-labels and out-of-distribution samples by aligning uncertainty distributions between labeled and unlabeled domains.
- Assumptions: Unlabeled data has similar distribution to labeled data; Uncertainty quantification can effectively identify problematic samples; Strong augmentations may introduce out-of-distribution samples

## Experimental Setup
- Datasets: CIFAR-10 (1000/4000 labels), SVHN (1000/4000 labels), CIFAR-100 (4000 labels), Mini-ImageNet (4000 labels)
- Baselines: MeanTeacher, MixMatch, ReMixMatch, FixMatch, SimPLE, Label Propagation
- Metrics: Top-1 Test Accuracy
- Compute: single GPU
