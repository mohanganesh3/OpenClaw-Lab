## Experimental Setup
- Datasets: Automated Cardiac Diagnosis Challenge (ACDC) dataset (200 annotated short-axis cardiac MR-cine images from 100 patients)
- Baselines: Unet, E-Net, Mean-Teacher (MT), Uncertainty-Aware Mean Teacher (UA-MT), Interpolation Consistency Training (ICT), Cross Pseudo Supervision (CPS), Uncertainty Rectified Pyramid Conistency (URPC)
- Metrics: Dice Coefficient (DSC), Hausdorff Distance 95 (HD95), Average Surface Distance (ASD)
- Compute: Single NVIDIA GTX 1080Ti GPU

## Critical Reading
- Stated limitations: The paper mentions that current methods struggle to balance computational cost, estimation accuracy, and theoretical support in a unified framework.
- Unstated weaknesses: The method is designed for segmentation rather than classification tasks; No explicit handling of label noise in unlabeled data is mentioned; No comparison with other uncertainty quantification methods is provided; The abstract is incomplete, limiting full assessment of the methodology
- Threats to validity: Domain shift from segmentation to classification may affect performance; Potential overfitting to specific medical imaging datasets; Limited information on theoretical guarantees of uncertainty estimation
- Open questions: How does EVIL specifically handle label noise in the unlabeled data?; What are the computational requirements compared to other methods?; How does it perform on classification tasks specifically?; What are the exact theoretical guarantees provided by the DST approach?
