## Problem
Current machine learning potentials struggle with uncertainty quantification, limiting their reliability for active learning, calibration, and out-of-distribution detection.

## Core Claim
Bayesian E(3)-equivariant MLPs with iterative restratification and joint energy-force uncertainty modeling achieve competitive accuracy while enabling reliable uncertainty quantification.

## Key Contributions
- Development of Bayesian E(3)-equivariant interatomic potential with iterative restratification of many-body message passing
- Introduction of joint energy-force negative log-likelihood (NLLJEF) loss function for simultaneous energy and force uncertainty modeling

## Method
Implements Bayesian neural networks with E(3)-equivariant message passing, using iterative restratification and NLLJEF loss to quantify uncertainties in both energies and forces simultaneously.
- Components: E(3)-equivariant message passing architecture, Iterative restratification of many-body interactions, Joint energy-force negative log-likelihood (NLLJEF) loss function, Uncertainty quantification via deep ensembles, SWAG, IVON, and Laplace approximation
- Key innovation: NLLJEF loss function enables simultaneous optimization of energy and force uncertainties, reducing energy errors by 3-4× and force errors by 2-3× compared to energy-only uncertainty modeling while maintaining predictive accuracy
- Assumptions: Uncertainty quantification is essential for reliable MLPs in active learning and OOD detection; E(3)-equivariance is necessary for accurate interatomic potential modeling; Joint energy-force uncertainty modeling provides complementary information
