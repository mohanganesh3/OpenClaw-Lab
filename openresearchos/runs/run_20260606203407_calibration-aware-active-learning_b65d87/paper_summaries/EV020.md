## Problem
Deterministic embeddings from standard Vision-Language Models struggle to capture uncertainties arising from ambiguities in visual and textual descriptions and multiple possible correspondences between images and texts.

## Core Claim
GroVE achieves state-of-the-art uncertainty calibration by providing a post-hoc approach to obtain probabilistic embeddings from frozen VLMs using Gaussian Process Latent Variable Models.

## Key Contributions
- GroVE as a post-hoc approach to obtain probabilistic embeddings from frozen VLMs
- Using GPLVM to learn a shared low-dimensional latent space for unified representation
- Optimizing through single-modal embedding reconstruction and cross-modal alignment objectives

## Method
GroVE builds on Gaussian Process Latent Variable Model (GPLVM) to learn a shared low-dimensional latent space where image and text inputs are mapped to a unified representation, optimized through single-modal embedding reconstruction and cross-modal alignment objectives.
- Components: Gaussian Process Latent Variable Model (GPLVM) framework, Single-modal embedding reconstruction objectives, Cross-modal alignment objectives
- Key innovation: The key innovation is using GPLVM to generate uncertainty-aware probabilistic embeddings from frozen VLMs through post-hoc optimization, enabling uncertainty quantification without retraining the original VLM.
- Assumptions: The frozen VLM representations can be effectively mapped to a shared low-dimensional latent space; The GPLVM can capture the underlying structure and uncertainties in the VLM embeddings; Single-modal reconstruction and cross-modal alignment objectives are sufficient for learning the latent space

## Experimental Setup
- Datasets: COCO, CUB
- Baselines: GroVE with different distance metrics
- Metrics: accuracy, uncertainty calibration
- Compute: computationally expensive compared to neural network-based methods

## Critical Reading
- Stated limitations: Existing approaches require large datasets and don't leverage pre-trained VLMs like CLIP; Deterministic embeddings from standard VLMs struggle to capture uncertainties
- Unstated weaknesses: Computational complexity of GPLVM may be prohibitive for large-scale medical imaging datasets; No explicit evaluation of calibration quality for uncertainty estimates; Potential domain shift issues when applying to medical images; No discussion of scalability to high-dimensional medical imaging data
- Threats to validity: Generalization to medical imaging domain may be limited; Active learning effectiveness not directly evaluated; Uncertainty quantification may not align with clinical decision-making needs
- Open questions: How does GroVE handle class imbalance common in medical datasets?; What is the computational overhead compared to deterministic embeddings?; How well does the method transfer to different medical imaging modalities?; Can the uncertainty estimates be directly used for active learning sampling strategies?

## Gaps This Enables
