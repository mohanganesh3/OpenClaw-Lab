## Problem
Deep Reinforcement Learning for quadrotor flight control typically relies on Domain Randomization for sim-to-real transfer, resulting in overly conservative policies that struggle with dynamic disturbances.

## Core Claim
We propose a novel adaptive control architecture that actively perceives and reacts to instantaneous perturbations through a Residual Dynamics Predictor and data-efficient calibration bridge.

## Key Contributions
- Training an optimal outer-loop policy and replacing its reliance on ground-truth disturbance data with a Residual Dynamics Predictor (RDP)
- Introducing a data-efficient linear calibration bridge and online thrust correction mechanism for seamless hardware transfer

## Method
The approach trains an optimal outer-loop policy, replaces ground-truth disturbance data with a Residual Dynamics Predictor that estimates 6D perturbations from state/action history, and uses a linear calibration bridge with online thrust correction for hardware transfer.
- Components: Residual Dynamics Predictor (RDP) using GRU networks, Linear calibration bridge for sim-to-real transfer, Online thrust correction mechanism
- Key innovation: The Residual Dynamics Predictor (RDP) enables adaptive control by estimating unobserved 6D environmental perturbations purely from temporal history of vehicle states and low-level actuation signals, allowing the controller to react to instantaneous disturbances without direct sensing.
- Assumptions: Symmetric cross quadrotor configuration with defined center-of-gravity offsets; Availability of state history and control action history for disturbance estimation; Ability to perform online calibration with minimal flight data

## Experimental Setup
- Datasets: quadrotor dynamics with payload variations
- Baselines: Base Controller, Robust Controller
- Metrics: RMSE, Max Error, Standard Deviation, Delay, Phase
- Compute: null

## Results
Headline: Adaptive Controller with RDP achieves RMSE of 0.024 m (0% payload)

- Ablations: RDP provides latent disturbance representation that accounts for modeling mismatches
- Improvement over baseline: Adaptive Controller with RDP reduces RMSE by up to 0.136 m compared to Base Controller
