## Abstract

Legal documents play a critical role in defining organizational processes, yet their complexity often hinders the derivation of accurate process models. Recent advances in Large Language Models (LLMs) provide an opportunity to automate this task through enhanced natural language understanding and generation. This study presents an LLM-based framework for generating and visualizing Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN) diagrams from legal texts. The proposed architecture integrates Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to address token limitations while ensuring actor-aware and scalable workflow generation. Experimental evaluation on Indonesian General Election Law (UU Pemilu No. 7/2017) and the Criminal Procedure Law (KUHAP, UU No. 8/1981) shows that the GPTbased approach outperforms Gemini, achieving over 92 % in accuracy, precision, and recall, with significantly reduced processing time. The key contribution lies in bridging legal text and BPMN workflows through an LLM-driven pipeline. Current limitations include handling complex conditional gateways, while future work will focus on enhancing reasoning for conditional flows and optimizing the BPMN engine layout so that diagram dimensions (width and height) dynamically adjust to the generated text, thereby improving readability and usability.
