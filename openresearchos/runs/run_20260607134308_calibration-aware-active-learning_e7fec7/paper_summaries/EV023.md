## Problem
Learning with one-hot labels in deep learning leads to overconfident predictions and poor robustness, while existing solutions like label smoothing fail to account for sample-specific ambiguity.

## Core Claim
Leveraging quantum non-determinism through variational quantum circuits can generate more nuanced, human-like uncertainty representations in labels, improving robustness and calibration in image classification.

## Key Contributions
- A hybrid quantum-classical framework using VQC to extract probabilistic soft labels from quantum measurements
- Demonstrating up to 50% higher accuracy under noise while maintaining competitive clean-data accuracy
- Enhanced model calibration and interpretability with CNN outputs better reflecting quantum-derived uncertainty

## Method
The method employs a variational quantum circuit (VQC) to encode each input image into a multi-qubit quantum state using entanglement and superposition. The quantum state is measured via the Born rule to extract a probabilistic soft label distribution that reflects input-specific uncertainty, which then trains a classical CNN with soft-target cross-entropy loss.
- Components: Variational quantum circuit (VQC) that encodes inputs into quantum states, Born rule measurement to extract probabilistic soft labels, Classical CNN training with soft-target cross-entropy loss
- Key innovation: Using quantum non-determinism through VQC to generate sample-specific probabilistic labels that capture intra-class variability and genuine ambiguity, unlike uniform label smoothing.
- Assumptions: The VQC can effectively encode image inputs into quantum states that capture relevant feature correlations; Quantum measurements via the Born rule can produce meaningful probabilistic labels; The quantum-derived labels provide better supervision signals than one-hot labels for training classical CNNs
